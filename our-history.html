OUR ROOTS

The Redeemed Christian Church of God

A brief history of the global ministry we are privileged to be part of

Founding & Early Years

The Redeemed Christian Church of God (RCCG) was founded in 1952 by Reverend Josiah Akindayomi in Lagos, Nigeria. Reverend Akindayomi, who was illiterate but had a supernatural ability to read the Bible, received a vision from God to start a church that would spread throughout the world.

The church started with just a few members meeting in a small house. Reverend Akindayomi prophesied that the church would grow to become a global ministry with branches all over the world. He led the church until his death in 1980.

Before his passing, Reverend Akindayomi received a divine revelation that his successor would be a young educated man named Enoch Adejare Adeboye, who had joined the church in 1973. This prophecy was fulfilled, and Pastor E.A. Adeboye became the General Overseer in 1981.

Rev. Josiah Akindayomi

Founder of RCCG (1909-1980)

Global Expansion Under Pastor E.A. Adeboye

When Pastor Enoch Adejare Adeboye assumed leadership in 1981, RCCG had about 40 parishes. A former mathematics professor, Pastor Adeboye brought organizational structure and strategic vision to the church while maintaining its spiritual foundation.

Under his leadership, RCCG has experienced exponential growth, expanding to over 198 countries and territories with more than 40,000 parishes worldwide. The church's vision "To make heaven, to take as many people with us, and to have a member of RCCG in every family of all nations" has been the driving force behind this global expansion.
